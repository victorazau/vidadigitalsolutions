## Step 1 — Set Up (or Optimize) Your Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the listing that shows up on Google Maps and in the sidebar panel when someone searches "house cleaning near me." Without a properly filled-out profile, you're completely invisible to people who are ready to hire right now.

**What you'll see on screen:** Go to [business.google.com](https://business.google.com), sign in with your business Gmail, and click "Manage now." If a profile already exists for your business, click "Edit profile."

### Required Setup Checklist:

1. **Business name** — use your actual business name without keyword-stuffing (e.g., "Miami Sparkle Cleaning" not "Best House Cleaning Miami")
2. **Primary category:** select *House Cleaning Service*; add secondary categories like *Maid Service* and *Commercial Cleaning Service* if applicable
3. **Address or service area** — if you travel to your clients, select "Service area business" and add every city you serve (e.g., Miami, Coral Gables, Doral, Hialeah)
4. **Phone number and website** — use a local number (not a 1-800 number) and link directly to your website
5. **Business hours** — include response hours, even if you don't take appointments on weekends
6. **Photos:** upload a minimum of 10 before-and-after photos, plus images of your uniforms, team, and vehicles. Profiles with 10+ photos receive 35% more clicks
7. **Business description:** write 250 words mentioning your city, services, and what sets you apart. Example: *"Miami house cleaning company serving Brickell, Coral Gables and Doral. Residential and move-in/move-out cleaning."*

**Pro tip:** Post an update on your GBP every single week — a photo with a caption or a current promotion. Google prioritizes active profiles over dormant ones.

## Step 2 — Automate Review Collection with GoHighLevel

Reviews are the second most important factor for local search rankings. The problem: most cleaning business owners are busy cleaning their clients' homes when they should be asking for feedback. That's exactly where GoHighLevel comes in.

**GoHighLevel is a CRM and automation platform** that lets you build automated message sequences. You set it up once, and it automatically requests a review from every client at exactly the right moment.

### How to Set Up the Review Automation in GoHighLevel:

**What you'll see on screen when you log into GoHighLevel:** a left-side navigation menu with options including *Conversations, Contacts, Opportunities, Automation, Sites*, and *Reputation*.

1. **Go to Automation → Workflows → + New Workflow**
2. **Choose a trigger:** select *"Appointment Status Changed"* and set the status to *"Completed"* — this fires the automation when a job is marked as finished
3. **Add a wait step:** insert a *Wait Step* of 2 hours (enough time for the client to get home and see the results of the cleaning)
4. **Add an SMS action:** click *"+ Add Action"* → *"Send SMS"* → write your message:
   > *"Hi [First Name]! Thank you for trusting us with your home 🏠 If you're happy with the service, we'd love a Google review — it takes 30 seconds and helps us a lot! [your Google Review link]"*
5. **Add an email follow-up:** 24 hours later, send an email with the same link in case the SMS didn't get a response
6. **Activate the workflow** by clicking *"Publish"* in the upper right corner

**What you'll see on screen in GoHighLevel Sites:** go to *Sites → Funnels & Websites → New Website*. Choose a local service template, swap out the photos and copy with your own, and publish it to your domain.

### Required Local SEO Elements on Every Page:

- **H1 heading** with your city: *"House Cleaning Services in Miami, FL"*
- **Opening paragraph** mentioning your city and neighborhoods: *"We serve Brickell, Doral, Coral Gables and surrounding areas"*
- **LocalBusiness schema markup** — ask your developer to implement this, or configure it in GoHighLevel Sites under *Settings → SEO Meta Data*
- **Consistent NAP** (Name, Address, Phone) that matches your GBP exactly across every page
- **Quote request button** visible above the fold — leads coming from SEO expect to be able to book quickly

## Step 4 — Set Up Automatic Responses to New Leads

Ranking on Google is only half the battle. If a potential client sends a message at 9 PM and you respond the next morning at 9 AM, they've already booked with your competitor. Industry data shows that 78% of service contracts go to the first company that responds.

Missed-call text-back is an automation that sends an automatic SMS whenever you miss a phone call — and it's one of the most popular GoHighLevel features among cleaning companies.

### How to Set Up Missed-Call Text-Back:

1. **In GoHighLevel, go to Settings → Missed Call Text Back**
2. **Enable the feature** and write your message: *"Hi! This is [Business Name]. Sorry we missed your call — we'd love to help! What day works best for a free quote? 😊"*
3. **For website form submissions:** go to *Automation → Workflows → New Workflow*, use the *"Form Submitted"* trigger, and add an immediate SMS plus a welcome email
4. **Automatically add the lead to your sales pipeline** — in *Opportunities*, create a *"New Lead"* stage and configure the workflow to move each new contact there automatically

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long does it take for a cleaning business to rank at the top of Google?**
**A:** With an optimized Google Business Profile, consistent reviews, and a locally-focused website, most cleaning companies start appearing in the top 3 of Google Maps within 60 to 90 days. Less competitive cities can see results in as little as 30 days; major metros like Miami or Los Angeles may take up to 120 days.

**Q: Do I need to run Google Ads to show up on Google?**
**A:** Not necessarily. Google Maps (organic local results) is free and depends on GBP optimization, reviews, and NAP consistency. Paid ads can accelerate visibility, but organic local rankings generate sustainable traffic with no ongoing cost per click.

**Q: Does GoHighLevel work for small cleaning businesses with 1 to 3 employees?**
**A:** Yes — and it's especially valuable for small operations where the owner does everything. GoHighLevel automates review requests, lead responses, and appointment follow-ups, freeing up hours every week so your business can grow without hiring a receptionist.

**Q: How do I respond to a negative Google review without hurting my reputation?**
**A:** Respond calmly, acknowledge the feedback, and offer a resolution: *"We're sorry to hear about your experience. Please contact us directly so we can make it right."* Never argue publicly. A well-handled negative review often convinces potential clients that your business is professional and trustworthy.

**Q: What's the difference between local SEO and traditional SEO for cleaning companies?**
**A:** Local SEO focuses on showing up for searches with geographic intent — *"house cleaning near me"* or *"cleaning service in Doral"* — and relies primarily on your Google Business Profile, reviews, and NAP consistency. Traditional (organic) SEO focuses on ranking website pages for broader search terms. For cleaning companies, local SEO delivers faster and more direct results.
