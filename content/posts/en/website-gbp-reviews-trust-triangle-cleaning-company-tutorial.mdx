## Step 2 — Set Up Your Google Business Profile the Right Way

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the listing that appears on Google Maps when someone searches *"house cleaning near me"* or *"residential cleaning in Austin."* It's **free**, and when properly set up, it's your most powerful marketing tool.

### How to Fill Out Each Field

**1. Business Name**
Use your actual business name. Don't stuff keywords like *"Austin Best Cleaning Service"* — Google penalizes this.

**2. Primary Category**
Choose **"House Cleaning Service"**. Add secondary categories: *"Janitorial Service"*, *"Maid Service"*, *"Carpet Cleaning Service"* (if applicable).

**3. Service Area**
In your GBP, you can hide your physical address and list only the cities you serve. Go to **Edit Profile → Business Location → Service Area** and add all your cities. This is essential for showing up in searches across different neighborhoods.

**4. Business Hours**
Fill these in accurately. GBPs with completed hours are 50% more likely to appear in local search results.

**5. Photos**
Add at least 10 photos: before/after cleaning shots, uniformed team members, equipment, and a professional cover photo. Profiles with photos receive **42% more direction requests** than profiles without them.

**6. Business Description**
Write up to 750 characters describing your services, service area, and what sets you apart. Include phrases like *"professional house cleaning"*, *"recurring cleaning service"*, *"move-in/move-out cleaning"*, and the names of the cities you serve.

**7. Weekly Posts**
Google prioritizes active GBPs. Create posts 1–2 times per week: promotions, cleaning tips, photos of completed jobs. GoHighLevel integrates with GBP to automate these posts — see Step 4.

## Step 3 — Build Your Review Strategy

Reviews are the #1 trust factor for cleaning service customers. Industry research shows that **87% of consumers** read online reviews before hiring a local service, and businesses with more than 50 Google reviews have a conversion rate up to **3x higher** than competitors with fewer than 10.

On top of that, AI tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity use reviews as a signal when recommending businesses. A profile with 80 reviews at 4.8 stars is far more likely to be cited by an AI than a profile with 5 reviews.

### How to Ask for Reviews the Right Way

**Timing is everything.** The best moment to ask for a review is **between 2 and 4 hours after the cleaning is completed** — when the client is still impressed with their spotless home and has the energy to respond.

**Never ask verbally without a link:** send a direct link to your GBP review page. To find your link, go to your Google Business Profile, click *"Ask for reviews"*, and copy the shortened link it generates.

**A message that works:**
> *"Hi [name]! Hope you're loving your clean home today 😊 Would you mind taking 1 minute to leave us a Google review? It really means a lot to us: [link]"*

Done manually, this works. Done automatically, it works at scale — and that's exactly what the next step covers.

## Step 4 — Automate Review Requests in GoHighLevel

This is where the trust triangle becomes self-sustaining. With GoHighLevel, you set up an automation that sends the review request automatically after every job — no reminders needed on your end.

### How to Set Up the Review Automation

**1. Go to Automations → + New Workflow**
In your GoHighLevel dashboard, click *"Automations"* in the left sidebar, then *"+ New Workflow"*. Select *"Start from Scratch"*.

**2. Set the Trigger**
Click *"+ Add Trigger"*. Select *"Appointment Status"* → *"Completed"*. This means: every time an appointment is marked as completed, the automation fires.

**What you'll see on screen:** A blue box with a calendar icon and the text *"Appointment Status is Completed"*.

**3. Add a 3-Hour Delay**
Click the *"+"* below the trigger → *"Wait"* → *"3 hours"*. This ensures the message arrives when the client is already home and satisfied.

**4. Add the SMS Action**
Click *"+"* → *"Send SMS"*. In the message body, use dynamic variables:
> *"Hi {{contact.first_name}}! Hope you loved your clean home today 😊 Would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It means the world to us: [your GBP link]"*

**5. Add a Follow-Up SMS (Optional)**
If the client hasn't left a review after 48 hours, add a second *"Wait"* of 2 days and a shorter follow-up SMS:
> *"Hi {{contact.first_name}}, just a gentle reminder 😊 Your review helps our small business grow: [link]"*

**6. Activate the Workflow**
In the top right corner, click *"Save"*, then toggle the switch from *"Draft"* to *"Published"*.

**What you'll see on screen:** The toggle turns green and the status changes to *"Active"*.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does my cleaning company need a website if I already have Instagram and a GBP?**
**A:** Yes. Instagram and GBP are third-party platforms — if they change their rules or take down your profile, you lose everything. Your own website is a permanent digital asset, ranks in organic Google search, and gives AI tools like ChatGPT more reason to recommend your business. All three channels work together — they don't replace each other.

**Q: How long does it take for a GBP to start showing up in local searches?**
**A:** A newly created or newly optimized GBP typically starts gaining positions within 4 to 8 weeks. The process speeds up with new reviews and regular posts. Profiles with 20+ reviews and recent photos tend to appear in the "Local Pack" (the top 3 Maps results) more quickly.

**Q: Can I ask all clients for reviews, or only the happy ones?**
**A:** Google allows you to ask all clients for reviews — what you **cannot** do is filter and only send the link to people you think will give you 5 stars (this is called "review gating" and violates Google's policies). Send the request to everyone, and respond publicly to negative reviews with professionalism.

**Q: Does GoHighLevel automatically send review requests to Google?**
**A:** Yes. GoHighLevel has a built-in module called *"Reputation Management"* that integrates with Google and sends automated review requests via SMS and email. You set up the workflow once and it runs for every future client with no manual input required.

**Q: What happens if a client doesn't have a cell number on file to receive the review SMS?**
**A:** GoHighLevel lets you configure the automation to try SMS first, and if no mobile number is available, automatically send the request by email instead. That's why it's important to always collect the client's email address at the time of booking.
