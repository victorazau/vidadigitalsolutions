## Step 1 — Optimize Your Google Business Profile in 20 Minutes

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the most powerful digital storefront for cleaning businesses in the US. It's what shows up on the map when someone types *"house cleaning near me"* into Google.

**What you'll see on screen:** Go to [business.google.com](https://business.google.com) and sign in with your Google account. You'll see a dashboard showing your business name, address, and verification status.

**Required actions:**

1. **Business name:** Use your exact company name — don't add keywords like "best" or "affordable" (Google penalizes this)
2. **Primary category:** Select *"House Cleaning Service"* or *"Janitorial Service"* — avoid generic categories
3. **Description:** Write 150–250 words describing your services, cities served, and what sets you apart. Include terms like *move-in cleaning, Airbnb cleaning, recurring cleaning*
4. **Photos:** Upload at least 10 real photos — before/after cleaning shots, uniformed team members, products you use
5. **Business hours:** Fill in every day of the week, including holidays
6. **Service area:** Add every zip code and city you serve (not just your home base)

> 💡 **Tip from Vida Digital Solutions:** Profiles with more than 10 photos receive 42% more direction requests than profiles with the bare minimum. Make it a habit to snap photos after every great job.

## Step 5 — Show Up in AI Search: ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini

In 2026, a growing share of local service searches starts on ChatGPT or Perplexity — not Google. When someone asks *"Who are the best house cleaners in Miami?"*, the AI scans websites, directories, and public data to generate its answer.

**How to get AI tools to recommend your business:**

**5.1 — Consistent directory listings**

Listing your business across major directories builds what's known as *NAP consistency* (Name, Address, Phone — identical across every platform). AI tools cross-reference this data to verify your business exists and assess its credibility.

Must-have directories:
- Google Business Profile
- Yelp
- Thumbtack
- Angi (formerly Angie's List)
- HomeAdvisor
- Facebook Business Page
- Nextdoor

**5.2 — Add a FAQ page to your website**

AI tools love FAQs because they're direct, self-contained answers. Create a dedicated page (or section) with questions your clients actually ask:

- *"How much does house cleaning cost in [city]?"*
- *"Do you bring your own cleaning supplies?"*
- *"How do I book a recurring cleaning?"*
- *"Are you insured and bonded?"*

Answer each question in 2–4 complete sentences. This is exactly the format ChatGPT extracts and cites.

**5.3 — Use descriptive, specific language on your website**

Instead of just *"We clean homes"*, write: *"[Company Name] is a licensed and insured house cleaning company serving [City], [City 2], and [City 3] in [State]. We offer recurring weekly and biweekly cleaning, deep cleaning, move-in/move-out cleaning, and Airbnb turnover cleaning."*

The more explicit and specific you are, the better AI tools understand your business — and the more likely they are to recommend you.
