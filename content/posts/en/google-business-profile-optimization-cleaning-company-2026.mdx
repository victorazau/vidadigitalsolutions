## The 5 Factors Google Uses to Rank Cleaning Profiles

Google's local algorithm evaluates three core dimensions: **relevance** (does your profile match what the person searched for?), **distance** (how close are you?), and **prominence** (how credible and active do you appear?).

Within those dimensions, here are the five most impactful factors specifically for cleaning companies:

**1. Correct Primary Category**
Use "House Cleaning Service" — not "Cleaning Service" or "Janitorial Service." Your primary category tells Google exactly which types of searches should trigger your profile.

**2. NAP Consistency (Name, Address, Phone)**
Your business name, address, and phone number must be identical everywhere your business appears online: GBP, your website, Yelp, Facebook, and local directories. Any inconsistency confuses the algorithm and hurts your ranking.

**3. Recency and Volume of Reviews**
Google prioritizes businesses with recent, ongoing reviews. A company with 50 reviews, all from 2023, will lose to a company with 20 reviews that receives 3 new ones per month in 2026. Frequency matters more than your total count.

**4. Profile Engagement**
When you respond to reviews, upload new photos, create posts, and add Q&A content, Google recognizes that the business is active. Abandoned profiles gradually lose their ranking position.

**5. Keywords in Your Description and Posts**
Including phrases like "house cleaning in [city]," "deep cleaning service [neighborhood]," and "move-out cleaning near [zip code]" in your description and posts signals relevance to specific local searches.
